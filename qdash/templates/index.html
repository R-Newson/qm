Welcome to Quality Management

Quality management in healthcare is a systematic approach to ensuring that healthcare services are safe, effective, patient-centered, timely, efficient, and equitable. It involves setting standards for care, measuring performance against those standards, and making improvements where necessary.

Quality, in the traditional sense, relates to whether a product or service satisfies the customer by meeting expected characteristics. However, in the context of healthcare, quality definition takes a more complex form. Not only must healthcare services satisfy the consumer – in this case, the patient – but they must also meet a stringent set of criteria to ensure they meet the highest possible standards and protocols. Indicators of quality are: Mortality Rate: This measures the percentage of patients who die while in the hospital’s care before being discharged. Safety Metrics: These indicators relate to medical incidents, including errors and adverse events. For example, tracking falls, infections, and medication errors. Hospital Readmission Rate: Monitoring how often patients return to the hospital shortly after discharge helps assess the effectiveness of care and patient outcomes. Patient Experience: Gathering feedback from patients about their satisfaction with care quality, communication, and overall experience. Timeliness and Effectiveness of Care: Evaluating wait times, timely diagnosis, and appropriate treatment delivery.
